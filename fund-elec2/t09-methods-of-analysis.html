Mesh Analysis

General Approach

Depending on the direction of current (Current flows from higher to lower potential), polarities are assigned to elements as follows:

For \[ \begin{array} a_1 I_1 + b_1 I_2 = c_1 a_1 I_1+ b_2 I_2 = c_2 \]

\[ \begin{align*} I_1 = \frac{ \begin{matrix} c_1 & b_1 c_2 & b_2 \end{matrix} }{ \begin{matrix} a_1 & b_1 a_2 & b_2 \end{matrix} } \\ I_2 = \frac{ \begin{matrix} a_1 & c_1 a_2 & c_2 \end{matrix} }{ \begin{matrix} a_1 & b_1 a_2 & b_2 \end{matrix} } \end{align*} \]

Here, as per KVL:

\[\begin{align*} E_1 - I_1 Z_1 - (I_1 - I_2) Z_2 = 0 \\ E_1 - I_1 Z_1 - I_1 Z_2 + I_2 Z_2 = 0 \\ E_1 - I_1 (Z_1 + Z_2) + I_2 Z_2 = 0 \\ \boxed{- I_1 (Z_1 + Z_2) + I_2 Z_2 = -E_1} \\ -(I_2 - I_1) Z_2 - I_2 Z_3 - E_2 = 0 \\ -I_2 Z_2 + I_1 Z_2 - I_2 Z_3 - E_2 = 0 \\ -I_2 (Z_2 + Z_3) + I_1 Z_2 = E_2 \\ \end{align*}\]

Now you have your equations, assign theme

Format Approach

For loop 1:

$ I_1 ( Z_x ) - I_2 ( Z_x ) = E_x $

For loop 2:

$ - I_1 ( Z_x ) + I_2 ( Z_x ) = E_x $
